What is Cyber Forensics?

Cyber Forensics (or digital forensics) is the practice of collecting, analyzing, and preserving electronic evidence from computers, networks, and digital devices. It is used in criminal investigations, civil litigation, and corporate security to reconstruct digital events and establish facts admissible in court.

Types of Cyber Crimes

Cyber crimes encompass a broad spectrum of offenses including phishing, ransomware, identity theft, DDoS attacks, cyber stalking, data breaches, and online fraud. Each type requires specialized investigation techniques and forensic methodologies to trace perpetrators.

Role of Digital Evidence

Digital evidence — including log files, emails, metadata, browser histories, and file system artifacts — forms the backbone of modern forensic investigations. Proper evidence handling follows a strict chain of custody to ensure admissibility, integrity, and authenticity in legal proceedings.

Network Analysis

Wireshark

The world's most popular network protocol analyzer. Wireshark captures live traffic and provides deep inspection of hundreds of protocols. Forensic analysts use it to detect anomalous traffic patterns, trace data exfiltration, and reconstruct network sessions during incident response.

Live Capture Deep Packet Inspection Protocol Decoding Traffic Filtering
Disk Forensics

Autopsy

An open-source digital forensics platform with a powerful GUI built on The Sleuth Kit (TSK). Autopsy enables investigators to analyze hard drives, smartphones, and media cards. Features include timeline analysis, keyword searching, web artifact extraction, and file carving for deleted data recovery.

Timeline Analysis File Carving Registry Analysis Hash Filtering
Imaging

FTK Imager

AccessData's FTK Imager is a data preview and forensic imaging tool used to acquire forensic images of local hard drives, CDs/DVDs, thumb drives, and network shares. It creates bit-for-bit copies (E01, DD, AFF formats) and generates hash values (MD5/SHA1) to verify evidence integrity.

Bit-for-Bit Imaging Hash Verification Evidence Preview Multiple Formats
Evidence Acquisition

Disk Imaging

Disk imaging is the foundational step in any forensic investigation. It creates an exact, sector-by-sector clone of a storage device, preserving all data — including deleted files, slack space, and unallocated clusters. Tools like dd, dc3dd, and FTK Imager are commonly used in the process.

Write-Blocking Sector Cloning Integrity Hashing Chain of Custody
Memory Forensics

Volatility

Volatility is the leading open-source framework for volatile memory (RAM) forensics. It extracts running processes, open network connections, loaded DLLs, registry hives, and encryption keys from memory dumps. Essential for detecting rootkits, malware, and evidence that exists only in volatile memory.

Process Analysis Malware Detection Network Artifacts Registry Extraction
Penetration Testing OS

Kali Linux — Download Guide

Kali Linux is a Debian-based distro designed for penetration testing and digital forensics. Follow these steps:

  1. Download ISO: Visit kali.org/get-kali and download the appropriate installer (64-bit recommended).
  2. Verify Checksum: Compare the SHA256 hash of the downloaded ISO against the official hash to ensure file integrity.
  3. Create Bootable USB: Use Rufus (Windows) or Etcher (macOS/Linux) to flash the ISO to a USB drive (≥ 8 GB).
  4. Boot & Install: Restart your machine, enter BIOS/UEFI, set USB as first boot device, and follow the guided installer.
  5. Post-Install: Run sudo apt update && sudo apt full-upgrade to ensure all packages are current.
  6. Virtual Option: Alternatively, download the pre-built VM image for VirtualBox or VMware from the same page.

Bug Bounty Basics

Bug bounty programs reward security researchers for finding and responsibly disclosing vulnerabilities. Major platforms include HackerOne, Bugcrowd, and Synack. Beginners should start with web application vulnerabilities (XSS, IDOR, SSRF) and practice on platforms like Hack The Box and TryHackMe to build skills before targeting live programs.

CFSL — Central Forensic Science Laboratory

CFSL operates under the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India, providing expert forensic analysis in criminal cases. Career roles include Scientific Assistant, Junior Scientific Officer, and Senior Scientific Officer. Recruitment is through SSC or direct CFSL notifications. A background in forensic science, chemistry, or biology is typically required.

BPR&D — Bureau of Police Research & Development

BPR&D is the think-tank of the Indian Police, focused on modernizing law enforcement through research and training. Forensic science professionals contribute to cybercrime research cells, training program development, and policy advisory for digital evidence handling. Positions are filled through UPSC and deputation.

INTERPOL — International Criminal Police Organization

INTERPOL coordinates cross-border cybercrime investigations through its Cyber Fusion Centre and Digital Forensics Lab. Career opportunities include Cyber Crime Analyst, Digital Forensics Specialist, and Incident Response Coordinator. Positions require advanced degrees, relevant certifications (GCFE, EnCE), and multi-language proficiency.
